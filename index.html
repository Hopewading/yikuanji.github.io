About Me

Welcome to my website! I am Yikuan Ji, a Ph.D. candidate in Agricultural and Resource Economics at University of Maryland, College Park. My research interests lie at the intersection of transportation economics, energy economics, and environmental economics. I plan to be on the 2025-25 Job Market.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my Master of Science in Applied Economics and Management from Cornell University and a Bachelor's degree in Public Economics from Fudan University, graduating with distinction. My academic journey has taken me from Shanghai to Honolulu, Ithaca, and now Maryland, enriching my perspective on urban transportation economics, with future plans to expand my research into broader economic issues.

My current research primarily focuses on the distributional effects of the New York City congestion charge policy, which also serves as my Job Market Paper. I analyze how this policy impacts various socioeconomic and geographic groups within the city. Additionally, my dissertation explores two related topics: the potential allocation of congestion charge revenue, emphasizing the role of public transit investments, and the effects of road construction on traffic congestion and urban mobility.
