About Me

Welcome to my personal academic website! I am Yikuan Ji, a Ph.D. candidate in Agricultural and Resource Economics at the University of Maryland, College Park. My research interests lie at the intersection of transportation economics, energy economics, and environmental economics. I am passionate about addressing urban transportation challenges and exploring how policy interventions, such as congestion pricing and public transit improvements, impact traffic congestion and environmental sustainability.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my Master of Science in Applied Economics and Management from Cornell University and a Bachelor's degree in Public Economics from Fudan University, graduating with distinction. My academic journey has taken me from Shanghai to Honolulu, Ithaca, and now Maryland, enriching my perspective on global economic issues.
