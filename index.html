About Me

Welcome to my website! I am Yikuan Ji, a Ph.D. candidate in Agricultural and Resource Economics at the University of Maryland, College Park. My research interests lie at the intersection of transportation economics, energy economics, and environmental economics. I am passionate about addressing urban transportation challenges and exploring how policy interventions, such as congestion pricing and public transit improvements, impact traffic congestion and environmental sustainability.

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned my Master of Science in Applied Economics and Management from Cornell University and a Bachelor's degree in Public Economics from Fudan University, graduating with distinction. My academic journey has taken me from Shanghai to Honolulu, Ithaca, and now Maryland, enriching my perspective on urban transportation economics, with future plans to expand my research into broader economic issues.

My research focuses on the economic analysis of urban transportation systems, including congestion pricing, the impact of road construction on traffic patterns, and the role of public transit in mitigating congestion. I am particularly interested in the distributional impacts of transportation policies and how they affect different socioeconomic and geographic groups. My work aims to inform policymakers in designing equitable and efficient transportation systems that promote sustainability and accessibility.
